ing Magazine

ing Magazine is an arts and culture magazine featuring Michigan State University and the surrounding East Lansing and Lansing community. It is produced by M3 Group, and currently edited by students in Kathryn Houghton's WRA 380: Editing and Publishing course. This semester, as an editorial staff member, I participated on the writing, developmental editing, copyediting and visuals teams. I collaborated with the rest of the editorial team to make decisions about featured stories and the production schedule, as well as communicating with freelancers. We strove to write articles in a journalistic style, featuring interviews and quotes, and following AP Style. I wrote two articles that were published in the November 2014 issue: "NAISO Mantains Cultural Connections at MSU" and "The Joy of Letter-Writing." For the December 2014/January 2015 issue, I wrote one article, "The Life of a Nontraditional MSU Student," as well as contributing to the Editors' Note.

Classical Rhetoric Website

As an honors option for my WRA 260 course on Rhetoric, Persuasion, and Culture, I worked with a small team to create a central Weebly website to host our class research projects on classical rhetoric. Our goal was to explore how different cultures used classical rhetorical proofs (logos, pathos, and ethos) to build credibility, trust, logic, and emotional connection. We used interviews and scholarly research to draw our conclusions, and also created podcasts. As well, we researched and outlined how our websites could be used to teach classical and cultural rhetoric in a variety of undergraduate courses. Classical rhetoric is still useful today because in making any piece of writing effective, it's essential to consider the piece's audience, context and purpose.

Copyediting

I have gained experience as a copyeditor through my WRA 370: Grammar and Style course. In this course, we developed a strong background in English grammar and punctuation, learning to follow Chicago Style and make good use of references such as "The Chicago Manual of Style" and "The Copyeditor's Handbook." We learned how to collaborate with authors, write queries and memos, and use good communication practices. We edited documents, both manually using proofreaders' marks and digitally using track changes in Microsoft Word. We learned correct citation formatting in both Chicago Author-Date Style and Chicago Notes & Bibliography Style.As well as learning to proofread and copyedit with precision, we each developed a personal editing philosophy and approach to our work. My personal editing philosophy is to maintain the author's unique voice while ensuring clarity for the reader.
