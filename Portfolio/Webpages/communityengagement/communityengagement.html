Community Engagement

Center for Poetry

For the last year and a half, I have been an intern at the RCAH Center for Poetry, an organization dedicated to creating "an awareness of the place and power of poetry in our everyday lives." Currently, I am an education coordinator, and my work focuses on community outreach. I plan and lead creative writing workshops for elementary and middle school students and for senior citizens, as well as attending Community Council meetings. I have led an assembly for 6th-8th graders at the Lansing STEM Academy, as well as bringing poetry nights and a kids' creative writing club to Edgewood Village in East Lansing. In my work, I focus on making writing accessible to everyone through intriguing activities; I believe everyone has the power to express themselves in a compelling way through writing. As well, I assist with day-to-day tasks at the Center, such as helping run events with visiting writers and promoting our events on social media. I have included a link to the Center's website, and to a blog post I wrote for our Wordpress about teaching at STEM Academy.

Community Research in Costa Rica

In Summer 2013, I participated in a seven-week study abroad program in Costa Rica through the Residential College in the Arts and Humanities. Our program was called, "Ethics of Tourism and Community Development," and focused on learning how small communities were developing rural tourism projects that aligned with their communities' goals and values. On the trip, we spent the first four weeks living in homestays and attending intensive Spanish classes at Conversa, a language school in Santa Ana. This helped us gain the proficiency we would need in our rural community placements, where we spent the second month of our trip. Four classmates and I went to Santa Rosa-Cartagena, a small community near Guapilés in the province of Limón. Here, we stayed with families, worked with members of a new agricultural cooperative, and taught ELL classes to elementary school students and community members. We each conducted original research, through interviews in Spanish, that was compiled by our professors into a report for the community. I researched issues and challenges facing women in the community, namely access to education and employment.

Roial Writers

This is my second year as leader of Roial Writers, a student creative writing group at Michigan State University. We are the writing branch of Roial Players, a student theatre organization. As leader, I bring creative writing activities and prompts to meetings, and help facilitate workshops of student-written pieces, where we provide comments for revision in order to make someone's piece the best that it can be. It's important to create an open and trusting environment where members feel comfortable contributing their opinions. Our group helps with the editing process of student-written plays that are performed by the organization. Last year, I worked closely with a social work student to help her develop and edit "Blinded," a play examining domestic violence. The play was produced in April 2014.
