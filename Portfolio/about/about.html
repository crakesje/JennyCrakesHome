About

At Michigan State University, I am double-majoring in Arts and Humanities and Professional Writing, and minoring in French. In my professional writing program, I follow the editing and publishing track. I am an education coordinator with the RCAH Center for Poetry, and an intercultural aide with the Office of Cultural and Academic Transitions. My home is in British Columbia, Canada, and I love to travel. In summer 2013, I had a wonderful experience studying abroad in Costa Rica with my residential college, on the program "Ethics of Ecotourism and Community Development"; I have also worked at a Girl Scouts camp in Rhode Island for the past four summers. My other interests include creative writing, theatre, dance, and the outdoors.

I work in a variety of different fields within writing. I consider myself very much a creative writer: while playwriting is my greatest passion, I also write fiction, nonfiction, and poetry. My professional writing skills include experience with developmental and copyediting, practice using AP and Chicago Style, and time on the editorial staff of ing Magazine. Much of my community work has taken place during my internship at the RCAH Center for Poetry, where I am an Education Coordinator. I am committed to working directly with community members, in settings including schools and housing centers. Active engagement with others enhances the meaning of any field.
