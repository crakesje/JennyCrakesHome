Plays In Performance

I am passionate about playwriting, due to the potential of plays to bring stories to life. Two of my plays have been produced here at MSU. My one-act play "The Runaways" tells the story of two sisters at one of the U.S. Government's Indian Boarding Schools that sought to assimilate Native American youth into White society. I co-directed the play with MSU's student theatre club Roial Players in March 2013. My ten-minute play "Letterpress," inspired by the story of Sophie Scholl and the White Rose resistance in Nazi Germany, was produced by the MSU Department of Theatre's Student Playwriting Series in April 2014. Currently I am developing a full-length play, "Golden Land," about Russian Jewish immigrant sisters, as part of my senior thesis in the Arts and Humanities.

I have included a link to a description of my play "The Runaways" on the RCAH website. For my script, I won 3rd place in the Martin Luther King, Jr. Advancing Inclusion Through Research Award.

The April Project Blog

In April 2014, I created a Wordpress blog for National Poetry Month. Each day, I posted an excerpt of a poem that is meaningful to me, as well as a short new poem I wrote that day. Paying attention to inspiring details helped me experience each day more fully, and I enjoyed sharing new poems with othes who might enjoy them.

RCAHive Magazine

Since sophomore year, I have written poems and stories for RCAHive, the student literary magazine for the Residential College in the Arts and Humanities. On staff, I have held the positions of Features writer, Arts writer, and Assistant Editor. I have attached a link to one of the poems published in RCAHive, which won the 2014 Balocating Prize for Poetry.
